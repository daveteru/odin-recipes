Carbonara

Description

Carbonara is a classic Italian pasta dish, specifically from Rome, known for its creamy sauce made with eggs, hard cheese (like Pecorino Romano or Parmesan), cured pork (often guanciale or pancetta), and black pepper. The dish is characterized by the rich, silky sauce that coats the pasta, achieved by emulsifying the eggs and cheese with the hot pasta and rendered fat from the pork. It's important to note that authentic carbonara does not traditionally include cream.

Ingredients

  • 200g spaghetti (or rigatoni)
  • 100g guanciale (pork cheek), cut into short strips
  • 2 large egg yolks + 1 whole egg
  • 50g Pecorino Romano cheese, finely grated
  • Freshly cracked black pepper
  • Salt (for pasta water)

Steps
